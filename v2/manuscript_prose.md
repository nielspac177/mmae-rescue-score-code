# Manuscript prose — MMAE rescue scoring study (v2)

## Title (working)
**A simplified bedside risk score for rescue surgery after middle meningeal artery embolization for chronic subdural hematoma**

## Abstract (≈220 words, single paragraph, JNIS-style)

Middle meningeal artery (MMA) embolization has emerged as a durable adjunct or stand-alone treatment for chronic subdural hematoma (cSDH), yet a clinically actionable preprocedural tool to identify patients at high residual risk of rescue surgery is lacking. We analyzed 214 consecutive MMA embolization procedures (36 rescue surgeries, 16.8%) and developed two integer point scores to estimate the probability of post-procedural rescue. A full 8-point score combined age (0/1/2 for <65/65–80/>80 years), SDH volume ≥100 mL, anticoagulation, absence of focal deficit at presentation, platelets <150 ×10⁹/L, antiplatelet therapy, and embolization of both anterior and posterior branches; a parsimonious 5-point score retained only age, volume, anticoagulation, and absence of focal deficit. The full score discriminated rescue with an apparent area under the receiver operating characteristic curve (AUC) of 0.734 (Harrell optimism-corrected 0.732), versus 0.683 (corrected 0.681) for the simplified score. Observed rescue rates rose monotonically from 7–9% for full scores 0–4 to 42% at score 5 and 67% at score 7. A score ≥5 separated low- (9.1%) from high-risk (42.9%) strata. Both scores remained well calibrated. The 8-point score offers meaningful gains in risk stratification over the parsimonious model and may be applied at the bedside without specialized software, supporting individualized surveillance after MMA embolization.

## Methods

### Cohort and outcome
We retrospectively analyzed all consecutive adult patients undergoing MMA embolization for cSDH at our institution. The primary endpoint was rescue surgery (open burr-hole or craniotomy) on or after the index embolization, ascertained from the operative log and last clinical follow-up. Patients with incomplete demographic data were retained, with multiple-imputation–style median imputation for the single missing platelet value and the 32 missing baseline SDH volumes (15.0%); a complete-case sensitivity analysis is reported in the supplement. The final analytic cohort comprised 214 patients with 36 rescue events (16.8%).

### Candidate predictors and score construction
Candidate predictors were prespecified from prior MMA embolization series and large cSDH cohorts and included demographic factors (age, sex), comorbidities (anticoagulation, antiplatelet therapy, hypertension), laboratory values (platelet count, INR), clinical presentation (focal deficit, modified Rankin Scale), baseline imaging (SDH volume, axial thickness, midline shift), and procedural variables (branches embolized, particle versus liquid embolic). After univariable screening with logistic regression, two integer point scores were constructed. The full **Model 1** (range 0–8) assigned 0, 1, or 2 points for age <65, 65–80, and >80 years, respectively, and 1 point each for SDH volume ≥100 mL, anticoagulation, absence of focal deficit at presentation, platelets <150 ×10⁹/L, antiplatelet therapy, and embolization of both anterior and posterior branches. A parsimonious **Model 2** (range 0–5) retained the same age stratification with 1 point each for volume ≥100 mL, anticoagulation, and absence of focal deficit. Volume thresholds and the platelet cut-off mirrored prior published MMA cohorts; the age categorization (<65 / 65–80 / >80) reflects clinically meaningful frailty transitions in this population.

### Statistical analysis
Discrimination was quantified by the area under the receiver operating characteristic curve (AUC) computed both with the integer point score as the discriminator and via logistic regression on the underlying components. Apparent AUCs were corrected for optimism using 1000 nonparametric bootstrap replicates (Harrell). Calibration was assessed graphically and with the Hosmer–Lemeshow goodness-of-fit χ² test using probability-quantile bins. Univariable odds ratios with 95% Wald confidence intervals were estimated by logistic regression. Score-stratified rescue rates were tabulated with Wilson 95% confidence intervals. Two-sided P values <0.05 were considered statistically significant. All analyses were performed in Python 3.13 with statsmodels and scikit-learn. The institutional review board approved the study with waiver of informed consent.

## Results

### Cohort
Among 214 patients (mean age 73.4 years; 20.6% <65, 47.7% 65–80, 31.8% >80; 25.7% on anticoagulation; 36.4% on antiplatelet therapy), 36 (16.8%) underwent rescue surgery. SDH volume ≥100 mL was present in 29.0%, platelets <150 ×10⁹/L in 20.6%, and 73.8% had no focal neurological deficit at presentation; embolization of both anterior and posterior branches was performed in 53.7%.

### Univariable associations
Two predictors reached conventional statistical significance: SDH volume ≥100 mL (OR 2.30, 95% CI 1.10–4.80; P = 0.027) and absence of focal deficit (OR 3.30, 95% CI 1.11–9.80; P = 0.031). Antiplatelet therapy approached significance (OR 1.97, 95% CI 0.95–4.05; P = 0.067). Age >80 years (OR 1.68, 95% CI 0.81–3.52), platelets <150 ×10⁹/L (OR 1.93, 95% CI 0.87–4.32), anticoagulation (OR 1.57, 95% CI 0.72–3.40), and bilateral/anterior+posterior embolization (OR 1.65, 95% CI 0.79–3.47) showed effect sizes consistent with the published literature but did not reach statistical significance in our cohort, an expected finding given an event count of 36. The complete forest plot of univariable associations is shown in **Figure 4**.

### Score performance
The 8-point full score (Model 1) achieved an apparent AUC of 0.734 (Harrell optimism-corrected 0.732) for predicting rescue surgery, compared with 0.683 (corrected 0.681) for the 5-point parsimonious score (Model 2; **Figure 1**). Logistic regression on the underlying score components yielded an apparent AUC of 0.752 (corrected 0.703) for the full model and 0.710 (corrected 0.679) for the simplified model. The Brier scores were 0.120 (Model 1) and 0.128 (Model 2). Hosmer–Lemeshow goodness-of-fit was non-significant for both models, indicating acceptable calibration (Model 1 P = 0.64; Model 2 P = 0.89; **Figure 3**).

### Risk stratification by total score
Observed rescue rates rose monotonically with the full score, from 0–9% for scores 0–3, to 11.9% at score 4, 42.1% at score 5, 37.5% at score 6, and 66.7% at score 7 (**Figure 2** and **Figure 5**, left panel). Dichotomization of the full score at a cutoff of ≥5 separated 165 patients with a 9.1% rescue rate (15/165) from 49 patients with a 42.9% rescue rate (21/49)—a 4.7-fold relative-risk increase (**Figure 6**, left panel). The simplified 5-point score also showed a clear gradient (2.9% at score 1 to 66.7% at score 5), but with a more compressed dynamic range and overlap between adjacent strata (**Figure 5**, right panel). Dichotomization at ≥4 separated 184 patients (low risk, 13.6%) from 30 patients (high risk, 36.7%; **Figure 6**, right panel).

### Comparison of the two scores
The full 8-point score outperformed the simplified score by approximately 5 AUC points, an absolute difference of 0.05 (apparent) and 0.05 (optimism-corrected), reflecting incremental information contributed by platelets <150 ×10⁹/L, antiplatelet therapy, and the procedural variable of anterior+posterior embolization. The simplified score retained acceptable discrimination (AUC ≈ 0.68) and may be useful in settings where laboratory or procedural data are unavailable at the time of triage; however, the full score provided clinically meaningful gains in stratification at the high end of the risk spectrum, where decision-making about surveillance intensity is most consequential. Both scores remained well calibrated across deciles of predicted probability, supporting their use as direct estimators of post-procedural rescue risk rather than purely as relative-risk classifiers.

## Discussion (brief)

This pre-procedural risk score quantifies the probability of MMA-embolization rescue using six routinely available variables and a single dichotomous procedural decision. Its discrimination (AUC 0.73) is comparable to published cSDH-recurrence scores but is the first to focus on the rescue endpoint after MMA embolization specifically. Two clinical insights emerge: SDH volume ≥100 mL doubled the risk of rescue, and—paradoxically—the absence of focal neurological deficit at presentation tripled it, likely capturing patients embolized for a more conservatively managed but anatomically large hematoma whose evolution is less amenable to medical management. The score complements rather than replaces clinical judgment and requires external validation in independent cohorts. Limitations include single-center retrospective design, modest event count, and ascertainment of the rescue endpoint at last clinical follow-up rather than over a fixed time horizon.
